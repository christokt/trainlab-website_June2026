Dr. Christo Kurisummoottil Thomas

Assistant Professor, Worcester Polytechnic Institute

Dr. Thomas completed his postdoctoral research at Virginia Tech and earned his Ph.D. from EURECOM, France. He holds an M.S. from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, and a B.S. from NIT Calicut.

His research interests include semantic communications, statistical signal processing, and machine learning for wireless communications. Before academia, he contributed to 4G LTE, 5G modem design, and advanced wireless R&D at Broadcom, Intel, and Qualcomm.

Albert Shaju

PhD Student in Electrical & Computer Engineering

Albert is a PhD student at WPI. He earned his B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Calicut. Prior to joining WPI, he gained research experience at Nokia, IIT Madras, and IIT Hyderabad.

His research interests include Next-Generation Wireless Networks (6G), AI-RAN, Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC), and Semantic Communications.
